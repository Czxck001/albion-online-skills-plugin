## The World of Albion

### What Kind of Game Is This?
Albion Online is a medieval fantasy **sandbox MMORPG** where:
- There are **no classes** — your gear defines your abilities ("you are what you wear")
- The **entire economy** is player-driven: every item is crafted, gathered, or won by players
- **Full-loot PvP** exists in dangerous zones — death has real consequences
- You write your own story: no quest chains, no linear path

### World Regions
- **The Royal Continent**: Safer, city-state governed areas. Great starting ground.
- **The Outlands**: Lawless territories controlled by guilds. Dangerous, best rewards.
- **The Mists**: Mysterious instanced realm accessible through open-world portals.
- **Roads of Avalon**: Hidden magical roads with unique dungeons, resources, and hideouts.
- **The Depths** *(new, June 2025)*: Ancient underground aqueducts beneath the cities,
  hosting Albion's newest instanced PvP mode. Accessible from every Royal City.

## New Player Roadmap (2025)

### Step 1: The Reworked Tutorial
As of the **Abyssal Depths update (June 2025)**, Albion has a completely reworked
new player experience. After the tutorial:
- You receive the **Albion Journal**, which now includes a **"First Steps" category**
  with guided goals and rewards
- Choose from **three starting Paths** reflecting different playstyles:
  - **Adventurer**: PvE-focused — dungeons, marketplace, open world
  - **Duelist**: PvP-focused — combat skills, early PvP gear progression
  - **Gatherer**: Gathering and fishing, minimal combat
- Old post-tutorial NPCs and quests have been removed in favor of this system

**Tip**: Any path works — you can pivot later. The Paths just guide your first week.

### Step 2: Reach Tier 4 Gear
T4 is the threshold where "the real game" begins:
- Most PvP and PvE content opens up at T4+
- Focus your Destiny Board on **one weapon family** and **one armor type**
- Don't spread learning points too thin early on

### Step 3: Make Your First Million
- Solo dungeons in blue/yellow zones are the most beginner-friendly silver source
- Or gather resources and sell them at the nearest Royal City
- Joining a guild early dramatically speeds up your learning

### Step 4: Try PvP (When Ready)
Albion's unique PvP ladder — escalating risk, escalating reward:
1. **Yellow zone** faction warfare (flagged PvP, no gear loss)
2. **The Depths** (Orange PvP — gear safe, inventory at risk — great PvP training)
3. **Non-lethal 2v2 Hellgates** (knockdowns, no gear loss)
4. **Corrupted Dungeons** (1v1 invasions, full loot)
5. **Lethal Hellgates** (full loot, team PvP)
6. **Red/Black zone open world** (full loot, highest risk)

### Step 5: Choose Your Path
- **PvP**: Corrupted Dungeons → Hellgates → ZvZ/GvG
- **Economy**: Crafting → Trading → Market flipping
- **Exploration**: Mists → Roads of Avalon → Outlands
- **Guild endgame**: Territory warfare, Crystal Arena, alliance politics

## Core Systems Explained

### The Destiny Board
Think of it like a massive skill tree — but instead of earning XP, you earn fame
(experience) by **actually using an item type**.

- Use a sword → unlock better swords
- Chop wood → unlock higher-tier gathering tools
- **Tip**: Focus one path first (e.g., T4 → T6 Swords before branching)
- Mastery and Specialization (the outer rings) increase your power with specific items
- **Learning Points (2025 update)**: Now delivered **daily after server maintenance**
  (changed in Abyssal Depths from continuous regen). Use them to skip the final 80%
  of a node — save for activities you do less often.

### Zone Colors & Risk (Updated 2025)

| Zone | PvP | Full Loot? | New in 2025? |
|------|-----|-----------|-------------|
| 🔵 Blue | None | No | Now has a soft IP cap for open world |
| 🟡 Yellow | Flagged only | No (flagged players) | — |
| 🟠 Orange | Mixed-initiative | No (equipped gear safe; inventory lost) | ✅ New (The Depths only) |
| 🔴 Red | Open PvP | Yes | — |
| ⬛ Black | Open PvP | Yes | — |

**Orange Zones** are a brand-new zone type introduced in the Abyssal Depths update,
exclusive to The Depths dungeons. They sit between Yellow and Red:
- Your equipped gear (weapon, armor, accessories) cannot be looted
- Your inventory (loot bags, consumables) drops on death
- Perfect for learning PvP without risking expensive gear

### The Depths *(New June 2025)*
Albion's newest content — instanced PvP dungeons under the Royal Cities.
- **Access**: Find Antiquarian's Dens — red glowing cracks near the Conquerors Hall
  in every Royal City (Caerleon: walkway into The Aqueducts)
- **Team size**: 2–3 players (or solo)
- **How it works**: Clear mob rooms, collect buffs, fight other player groups,
  escape before the dungeon collapses
- **Why it's great**: Best PvP training in the game — test expensive builds without
  losing them. Over 4 million runs in its first few weeks.

### Premium
Monthly subscription that significantly boosts progression:
- **2× Fame** from all activities (double XP effectively)
- **Unlocks Focus** — used to increase crafting/refining return rates
- **Labourers**: Hire NPC workers who passively gather resources for your island
- **Learning Points**: Delivered daily after server maintenance
- **Cost**: Buy with real money **or** with in-game silver (via Gold market)

**Is Premium worth it?** Yes, especially for crafting. Many players earn enough
silver in-game to buy Premium without spending real money.

### Islands (Player Islands)
Your personal private space. Purchase from any city's Island Manager NPC.

**What to build**:
- **Farming plots** → Crops for food buffs or profit
- **Animal pens** → Raise animals for hide, or baby animals (valuable on market)
- **Labourer cottages** → NPC workers who gather resources passively (premium)
- **Crafting buildings** → Private stations with no queue wait

### Mounts Quick Reference

| Mount | Speed | Best Use |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Horse | Standard | Everyday transport |
| Ox | Slow, high carry weight | Hauling large resource loads |
| Swiftclaw | Fast | Gathering escape mount |

*Note*: Baby Soulspinners are purchasable from the Antiquarian NPC using Favor earned
in The Depths — raise them into mounts on your island.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Albion Online free to play?**
A: Yes! Free to play, available on PC, Mac, iOS, Android, and Xbox. Premium is optional
but recommended for serious progression. You can buy Premium with in-game silver.

**Q: Is the game pay-to-win?**
A: Mostly no. Premium is the main paid advantage, and it's obtainable with silver.
Cosmetics are the primary real-money purchase beyond Premium.

**Q: Do I lose everything when I die?**
A: Only in Red and Black zones (full loot) and in The Depths (inventory only).
In Blue/Yellow zones, you keep your gear. This is why zone awareness matters.

**Q: What's the best build for beginners?**
A: No fixed classes — you wear what you want. For beginners:
- **Clarent Blade** (Sword): Versatile, decent damage, straightforward mechanics
- **Divine Staff** (Holy): Healing — always needed in groups
- **Warbow** (Bow): Ranged kiting — safer playstyle
Pair with **Scholar Robe** (mage DPS) or **Mercenary Jacket** (leather DPS).

**Q: What changed for new players in 2025?**
A: The Abyssal Depths update (June 2025) completely overhauled the new player experience:
- Reworked tutorial with three starting Paths (Adventurer/Duelist/Gatherer)
- New "First Steps" Albion Journal category with guided goals and rewards
- Old post-tutorial NPCs removed; cleaner, more structured onboarding
- The Depths added as a new, beginner-accessible PvP training ground

**Q: What city should I start in?**
A: Lymhurst or Fort Sterling are solid starting choices — active populations, good
access to various content. Caerleon has the most liquid market. You can always
relocate later using fast travel.

**Q: How do I make friends or find a guild?**
A: In-game guild search, Albion Online's official Discord, or Reddit (r/albiononline).
Many guilds actively recruit new players.
